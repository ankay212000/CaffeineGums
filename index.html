The foundation of any chewing gum is its precise blend of ingredients. This section breaks down the core components, their functions, and how they interact to create the final product. Click on the tabs to explore each category and see how they contribute to the overall formulation.

Gum Base (25-35%)

The non-digestible, chewable core that provides elasticity and texture. It's a complex blend of elastomers, resins, waxes, and fillers. The properties of the gum base are critical as they directly influence the release rate of caffeine and other active ingredients.

Sweeteners (40-50%)

Essential for palatability and masking caffeine's bitterness. This includes bulk sweeteners (like sorbitol, xylitol) for initial taste and mass, and high-intensity sweeteners (like sucralose) for prolonged sweetness. Encapsulation is often used for controlled release.

Caffeine (0.3-3.7%)

The active stimulant. Sourced from natural extracts (Green Tea, Guarana) or synthetically. It is often encapsulated or specially treated to mask its bitter taste and to control its release, providing either a quick boost or a sustained effect.

Softeners, Flavors & Colors (~5%)

These components fine-tune the sensory experience. Softeners (like glycerin) ensure flexibility. Flavors provide the desired taste profile and help mask bitterness. Colors enhance visual appeal. Their interaction is key to consumer acceptance.

Regulatory & Safety Checklist for Gum

Bringing a functional food product to market requires strict adherence to safety and regulatory standards. This checklist highlights the key compliance points from the report, covering FSSAI regulations in India, labeling laws, and essential safety testing.

📋

Compositional Standards (FSSAI)

Ensure the product meets specific limits for gum content, moisture, ash, and sugar content as defined by FSSAI regulations for chewing gum.

🔬

Caffeine Dosage & Limits

Carefully calibrate caffeine per piece (e.g., 40-100mg) to be effective yet safe, referencing FSSAI limits for caffeinated products and food supplements (e.g., max 300mg/day).

🏷️

Accurate Labeling & Claims

All functional claims must be scientifically substantiated. Labels must clearly state ingredient amounts, recommended daily limits, and include warnings for vulnerable groups (children, pregnant women).

🛡️

Safety & Stability Testing

Conduct mandatory microbiological safety testing and stability tests to ensure nutrient potency is maintained throughout the product's entire shelf life.

Procurement Checklist (Organic Sources)

Based on the report's findings, follow these key recommendations when sourcing organic dehydrated caffeine to ensure quality, safety, and compliance for your product.

🎯 1. Define Application Needs

Clarify desired caffeine concentration, release profile (immediate vs. sustained), and synergistic health benefits (e.g., L-theanine for focus).

📜 2. Verify Organic Certification

Request and scrutinize NPOP, USDA, or EU Organic certificates for the specific extract. Don't rely on vague "natural" claims.

🔬 3. Demand Comprehensive COAs

Insist on a Certificate of Analysis for every batch, detailing purity, contaminants, and microbial testing from a third-party lab.

💧 4. Assess Processing Transparency

Favor suppliers who use and clearly document "green" or water-based extraction methods and guarantee residue-free products.

⚠️ 5. Adhere to Safety Guidelines

Be aware of source-specific dosage limits and side effects (e.g., EGCG in green tea, drug interactions with guarana).

📞 6. Engage Directly with Suppliers

For B2B procurement, direct communication is crucial to confirm all technical specifications, certifications, and supply chain details.
